SECTION 00 // INTRODUCTION

This section provides an overview of mining operations, basic mechanics, and foundational knowledge required before proceeding to specific procedures.

What is Mining in Star Citizen?

Mining is a core gameplay loop in Star Citizen that allows operators to extract valuable minerals and ore from asteroids and planetary surfaces. The Prospector is a dedicated single-operator mining vessel designed for asteroid mining in space. Operators scan for valuable ore deposits, fracture rocks using a mining laser, and extract the ore into their cargo hold for refinement and sale.

Mining profitability depends on three factors: location selection, ore quality, and operational efficiency. This SOP focuses on maximizing all three to ensure consistent, high-yield mining operations.

Why the MISC Prospector?

The Prospector is the entry-level mining vessel in Star Citizen, designed for solo operators. It features a 32 SCU cargo hold, a front-mounted mining laser arm with interchangeable heads, and sufficient power and cooling systems for sustained mining operations. The Prospector is ideal for both new miners learning the mechanics and experienced operators looking for consistent profitability.

Prospector Advantages: Solo operation, low maintenance costs, proven profitability, accessible learning curve, versatile mining head options.

Basic Mining Mechanics

Phase 1: Scanning

Scanning is the process of detecting ore deposits within asteroids. Operators use the ship's scanner to ping the surrounding space for mineable rocks. The scanner returns signal strength based on distance and ore concentration. Stronger signals at greater distances indicate larger, more valuable deposits.

Key Scanning Principles:

  • Scan range: Approximately 10-15 km depending on equipment
  • Signal strength indicates ore quality and quantity
  • Bright, stable signals indicate mineable rocks
  • Faint or flickering signals indicate small, low-value rocks

Phase 2: Fracturing

Fracturing is the process of breaking down ore within a rock using the mining laser. The operator targets a rock and applies laser power to initiate a fracture. The fracture process involves managing a charge window and instability rating. When the charge reaches the green zone, the operator triggers the fracture, breaking the rock into extractable ore.

Key Fracturing Principles:

  • Charge window: The optimal power range for fracturing
  • Instability: Resistance to fracturing; higher instability = harder to crack
  • Gadgets and modules modify these parameters for better results
  • Timing and precision are critical to success

Phase 3: Extraction

Extraction is the process of collecting the fractured ore into the ship's cargo hold. Once a rock is successfully fractured, the ore is automatically collected. Operators monitor cargo capacity and manage the Quantanium timer (see below) to ensure safe extraction and transport to refineries.

The Quantanium Timer: Critical Safety Mechanic

Quantanium is the most valuable ore in Star Citizen, but it is highly volatile. Once Quantanium enters the ship's cargo hold, a 25-minute timer begins. If the timer expires before the ore is refined or sold, the Quantanium will explode, destroying the ship and all cargo.

CRITICAL: The Quantanium timer is absolute. Once started, it cannot be paused or reset. Operators must plan routes to refineries and manage cargo carefully to avoid catastrophic loss.

Timer Management Rules:

  • Timer begins when Quantanium enters cargo hold
  • Timer duration: 25 minutes from collection
  • Timer stacks with quantity (more Quantanium = higher risk)
  • Heat and damage reduce timer margin
  • Target cargo fill: 60-80% Quantanium (balances payout and survival)
  • Never attempt to "top off" cargo; return to refinery immediately when target is reached

Mining Glossary

Scan: The process of detecting ore deposits using the ship's scanner. Returns signal strength indicating ore quality and quantity.
Signal Strength: A measure of ore concentration and rock size. Stronger signals at greater distances indicate larger, more valuable deposits.
Fracture: The process of breaking down ore within a rock using the mining laser. Requires managing charge window and instability.
Charge Window: The optimal power range for fracturing. When laser power enters the green zone, the fracture is ready to trigger.
Instability: Resistance to fracturing. Higher instability means the rock is harder to crack and requires more power or gadget assistance.
Gadget: Mining equipment that modifies fracturing parameters. Examples: BoreMax (reduces instability), OptiMax (increases charge window), Stampede (increases laser power).
Module: Ship equipment that enhances mining performance. Examples: Surge modules (increase laser power), Focus modules (increase laser range).
Quantanium: The most valuable ore in Star Citizen. Highly volatile with a 25-minute timer before explosion. Requires careful management.
Extraction: The process of collecting fractured ore into the ship's cargo hold. Automatic once rock is fractured.
Refinery: Station facility where ore is processed into refined materials for sale. Processing time varies by ore type.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Ignoring Signal Strength
Chasing faint signals wastes time on low-value rocks. Always prioritize bright, stable signals at 8km+ distance. Faint signals inside 3-4km are usually not worth the effort.
Mistake 2: Mining Low-Percentage Ore
A full hold of low-quality ore (5-15% Quantanium) is worth less than a half-full hold of high-quality ore (30%+ Quantanium). Be selective. Skip rocks with less than 20% Quantanium unless desperate.
Mistake 3: Attempting Oversized Rocks
Rocks over 9,000 tons are extremely difficult for solo Prospector operations. A 12,000-ton rock with 40% Quantanium might seem tempting, but the time cost makes it unprofitable. Skip it and find smaller rocks.
Mistake 4: Ignoring the Quantanium Timer
The 25-minute timer is absolute. Never attempt to "top off" cargo or make detours. Return to refinery immediately when target fill is reached. Greed kills runs.
Mistake 5: Poor Power Management
Running out of power during fracturing leaves you stranded. Monitor power levels and manage power distribution carefully. Use power management gadgets and modules to optimize efficiency.
Mistake 6: Thermal Overload
Mining generates extreme heat. Insufficient cooling causes thermal damage and reduced laser efficiency. Ensure coolers are properly equipped and power-managed.
Mistake 7: Wrong Location Selection
Mining in contested or dangerous areas increases risk. New operators should start in safe, accessible locations. Experienced operators can take calculated risks in high-value zones.

SECTION 03 // STRATEGY

The 7-part ore selection framework for maximum profitability and safety.

Ore Selection Framework

This framework guides operators on which rocks to mine for maximum profitability. Follow each step in sequence to make optimal mining decisions.

1. Scan Return Strength: What to Chase

Signal strength indicates ore quality and rock size. Operators do not receive numeric values on the first ping, so use distance and clarity to evaluate signals.

Chase These Signals:

  • Return appears beyond ~8 km distance
  • Bright, stable blip on scanner
  • Does not flicker when you change heading
  • Often resolves into 2-4 rocks (cluster)
  • These correlate with mineable mass and profitability

Ignore These Signals:

  • Only appears inside 3-4 km distance
  • Faint or flickering signal
  • Resolves into a single pebble
  • These waste time with low yield

2. Rock Mass: The Hard Numbers

Once you scan a rock directly, you get mass in tons. Use these thresholds to determine if a rock is worth mining.

Sweet Spot (Fast Profit):

  • 4,000 – 7,500 tons mass
  • Ideal Quantanium range for solo Prospector
  • Fractures quickly, high yield, manageable time

Upper Practical Limit (With Your Setup):

  • ~8,500 – 9,000 tons mass
  • Crackable with Helix I + 2x Rieger C3 + at least one gadget
  • Time cost climbs fast above this threshold

Skip These:

  • <2,000 tons: Even if Quant is present, yield is too low
  • >10,000 tons: Not worth it solo. That is MOLE territory

3. Quantanium Percentage: When to Mine

Quantanium percentage is the real gate to profitability. This is the most critical decision point.

Mine Immediately If:

  • Quantanium ≥30% in a single rock
  • Quantanium ≥25% in a multi-rock cluster

Skip If:

  • Quant <20%
  • Quant mixed heavily with inert junk

Key Principle: A half-full hold of 35% Quantanium beats a full hold of garbage. Be selective. Greed reduces profitability.

4. Other Valuable Minerals

While Quantanium is the primary target, other minerals have significant value. Consider mining these if Quantanium is unavailable.

Mineral Min % to Mine Profitability Notes
Taranite ≥25% Very High Second most valuable ore
Hadanite ≥20% High Consistent value
Bexalite ≥15% Moderate Accessible, reliable
Corundum ≥10% Low-Moderate Filler ore, lower priority
Titanium ≥5% Low Minimal value, avoid if possible

5. Fracture Difficulty and Instability

You will see an optimal charge window and instability rating when scanning a rock. Use these to determine if fracturing is feasible.

Green Light (Proceed):

  • Instability manageable with BoreMax or OptiMax gadget
  • Charge window not razor-thin after gadgets applied
  • Fracture appears achievable with your setup

Red Flag (Skip):

  • Instability spikes uncontrollably
  • Charge window collapses even with gadgets
  • Fracture appears impossible with your setup

Rule: You are not here to wrestle rocks. Leave difficult rocks and find easier targets. Time is money.

6. Quantanium Timer: The Rule That Matters

Once Quantanium enters your hold, the 25-minute clock is live. This is absolute and non-negotiable.

What That Means in Practice:

  • You have ~25 minutes before explosion
  • Timer stacks with amount collected (more Quant = higher risk)
  • Heat and damage shorten margin

Hard Rules:

  • Start heading back once you hit your target fill
  • Do not "top off" cargo
  • Do not detour for one more rock

Target Fill (Safe and Fast):

  • 60–80% Quantanium
  • Balances payout and survival
  • Greed kills runs

7. Maximum Mass You Should Ever Crack Solo

Absolute Ceiling (With Perfect Execution):

  • ~9,000 tons mass
  • Requires Helix I, optimal gadgets, skilled operation

Smart Ceiling (Repeatable Profit):

  • ~7,500 tons mass
  • Consistent fractures, manageable time, reliable profit

The Temptation (Skip It):

  • If you see a 12k monster with 40% Quant:
  • Smile
  • Mark it mentally
  • Move on
  • You will make more credits skipping it

SECTION 07 // TROUBLESHOOTING

Solutions for common failures and emergency protocols.

Common Failures and Solutions

Failure 1: Laser Power Insufficient

Symptoms: Fracture charge level won't enter green zone. Laser power maxes out before reaching optimal charge.

Causes: Rock has high resistance. Laser power insufficient. Gadgets not equipped.

Solutions:

  • Equip Stampede module to increase laser power
  • Target rocks with lower resistance
  • Upgrade to Helix I mining laser (more power)
  • Upgrade power plant for sustained power output
  • Skip rock and find easier target

Failure 2: Instability Spikes Uncontrollable

Symptoms: Fracture charge level jumps erratically. Instability meter spikes uncontrollably.

Causes: Rock has high instability. Gadgets not equipped. Laser power not stable.

Solutions:

  • Equip BoreMax gadget to reduce instability
  • Feather laser power to maintain control (don't max power)
  • Avoid rocks with high instability ratings
  • Use OptiMax gadget to increase charge window
  • Skip rock and find easier target

Failure 3: Quantanium Timer Critical

Symptoms: Ship computer announces timer expiration imminent. Less than 5 minutes remaining.

Causes: Spent too much time mining. Didn't plan refinery route. Attempted to "top off" cargo.

Solutions:

  • IMMEDIATELY stop mining and head to refinery
  • Quantum jump directly to nearest refinery
  • Do not attempt additional mining runs
  • Do not detour for any reason
  • Dock and transfer ore to refinery immediately
CRITICAL: If timer expires before reaching refinery, your ship and all cargo will be destroyed. There is no recovery. Prioritize reaching refinery above all else.

Failure 4: Thermal Overload

Symptoms: Ship temperature exceeds 80°C. Coolers unable to manage heat. Laser efficiency reduced.

Causes: Coolers insufficient. Power not distributed to coolers. Mining laser running too long.

Solutions:

  • Reduce laser power immediately (50% instead of 100%)
  • Increase power to coolers (move slider to max)
  • Reduce mining laser duty cycle (mine less frequently)
  • Upgrade to IceBox cooler (Industrial A grade)
  • Upgrade power plant for better thermal management
  • Take break to allow ship to cool naturally

Failure 5: Power Plant Overload

Symptoms: Power plant shuts down. All systems offline. Ship dead in space.

Causes: Demanding too much power simultaneously. Power plant insufficient for setup.

Solutions:

  • Reduce laser power to 50% (frees up power for other systems)
  • Disable shields temporarily (frees up power)
  • Reduce cooler power (frees up power)
  • Upgrade power plant (Cirrus Industrial A for top-tier)
  • Manage power distribution more carefully

Failure 6: Shield Failure During Mining

Symptoms: Shields go down. Ship takes damage. Environmental hazards penetrate.

Causes: Insufficient shield power. Flying in hazardous zone. NPC attack.

Solutions:

  • Increase power to shields (move slider up)
  • Move away from hazardous zone
  • Evade NPC attackers (boost away, quantum jump)
  • Upgrade shield generator (7SA 'Concord' for top-tier)
  • Avoid high-risk zones if shields are weak

Failure 7: Cargo Hold Overflow

Symptoms: Cargo hold full. Cannot collect more ore. Mining laser stops working.

Causes: Collected too much ore. Didn't plan cargo capacity.

Solutions:

  • Stop mining immediately
  • Quantum jump to refinery
  • Transfer ore to refinery
  • Return to mining zone
  • Plan cargo capacity better (target 60-80% fill for Quantanium)

Failure 8: Navigation/Approach Vector Failure

Symptoms: Cannot approach rock. Laser won't target. Ship stuck in asteroid.

Causes: Approach angle too steep. Asteroid blocking path. Navigation error.

Solutions:

  • Adjust approach vector (aim for 45-degree angle)
  • Reduce speed to 0 m/s
  • Boost away from asteroid and re-approach
  • Select different rock with better approach
  • Practice approach technique

Failure 9: Environmental Hazards (Cold, Radiation)

Symptoms: Ship takes environmental damage. Shields flicker. Systems malfunction.

Causes: Mining in extreme environment. Insufficient shielding. Prolonged exposure.

Solutions:

  • Move to safer zone with less extreme environment
  • Increase shield power for better protection
  • Reduce time in hazardous zone
  • Upgrade shields and coolers for better protection
  • Avoid extreme environment zones until experienced

Failure 10: Emergency: Ship Damage/Distress

Symptoms: Hull integrity critical. Life support failing. Systems offline.

Causes: Combat damage. Environmental damage. Collision.

Solutions:

  • Quantum jump to nearest station immediately
  • Disable all non-essential systems to conserve power
  • Reduce speed to 0 m/s
  • Activate distress beacon if available
  • Call for assistance if in group
  • Attempt emergency landing at nearest station
EMERGENCY PROTOCOL: If ship is critically damaged, prioritize reaching nearest station for repairs. Abandon mining operation. Cargo is not worth your life.
